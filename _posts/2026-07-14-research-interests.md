My interests currently connect three areas: communications, signal processing, and machine learning. This post records the questions that make those areas interesting to me.

## Communications

Communication systems turn physical signals into reliable information. I am interested in how modern wireless systems balance mathematical models, hardware constraints, limited spectrum, and changing environments.

## Signal Processing

Signal processing provides the language for finding structure in noisy measurements. Topics such as estimation, detection, filtering, and spectral analysis offer a strong mathematical foundation for both communications and data-driven methods.

## Machine Learning

Machine learning adds flexible models that can learn patterns which are difficult to specify manually. I am especially interested in when learned models improve a communication or signal-processing pipeline, and when domain knowledge should remain explicit.

## A Working Direction

For now, the goal is to build foundations rather than claim a narrow specialization. I plan to study the mathematics carefully, implement small reproducible experiments, and use the results to identify better research questions.
